C. Whole Solution Framework (9-Block DSA Template)
1. Problem Framing: In 2026, the "10 blue links" era is over. Users now receive direct answers from AI Overviews and chatbots, causing a 25-40% decline in click-through rates (CTR) for informational queries. If your content is not structured to be cited rather than clicked, you are invisible.
2. Root Causes:
• LLM Summarization: AI absorbs Top-of-Funnel (TOFU) traffic.
• Entity Reliance: Search engines now prioritize "Entities" (Brands/Concepts) over keywords.
• Trust Deficit: The flood of AI content has made Google prioritize "Hidden Gems" (forums/video) over generic blogs.
3. Mechanisms (How It Works):
• Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG): AI tools search Google/Bing, read the top results, and synthesize an answer. They prioritize sites with clear structure, data tables, and citations.
• Citation Probability: Sites with unique data, strong schema, and "first-person experience" are mathematically more likely to be cited.
• Cross-Platform Signals: Google correlates brand mentions on YouTube and LinkedIn with website authority to validate legitimacy.
4. Common Missteps:
• Spamming Reddit: Careless "parasite" posts are being de-indexed and accounts banned.
• Keyword Stuffing Entities: Robotically inserting entity names decreases readability and engagement signals, hurting rank.
• Ignoring Video: YouTube is often the most clicked source in AI results, yet many ignore video SEO.
5. Protocol Steps (The Solution):
• Step 1: Entity Definition: Define your brand clearly using Semantic Triples (Subject-Predicate-Object) on your about page.
• Step 2: BOFU Focus: Pivot content strategy to Bottom-of-Funnel (Commercial) intent where AI cannot satisfy the user without a click (e.g., "Buy," "Book," "Compare").
• Step 3: Multi-Channel Syndication: Repurpose one core solution into a YouTube video, LinkedIn Article, and structured Blog Post simultaneously.
• Step 4: Schema & Data: Implement JSON-LD schema and include unique data tables in every article.
6. Stacks (Tools):
• Research: Perplexity (for intent discovery) + Google Search Console (Commercial Regex filtering).
• Content: Harbor SEO / Arvo (for structured drafting) + Claude (for refinement).
• Technical: RankMath Pro (Schema) + Screaming Frog (Internal Link Audit).
7. Objections:
• "I don't have time for video." Response: Use tools like Riverside or Opal to auto-generate video/clips from transcripts.
• "AI answers steal my traffic." Response: AI traffic converts at 5x the rate of cold search traffic because the user is pre-qualified by the bot.
8. Proof (Citations):
• Google's AI Overviews merge with AI Mode (Gemini 3 Flash).
• HubSpot's traffic decline due to weak topical authority.
• Ahrefs study: 25-40% CTR decline for sites not cited in AI Overviews.
9. Next Action: Run the Module 7 Site Audit to identify your BOFU gaps and Topical Authority deficiencies immediately.

MODULE 1: geo-strategy-architect
• Purpose: Directive strategy. Defines where to play (Google vs. YouTube vs. Perplexity).

    1. Analyze business model & ICP.
    2. Map "Platform Strategy Matrix" (e.g., if B2B, heavy LinkedIn/YouTube focus).

• Purpose: Orchestration/Research. Finds the money keywords.
• Workflow:
    1. Export GSC data using Regex for commercial intent (buy, best, review, vs).
    2. Use Perplexity to find "Related Questions" and "Competitor Citations".
    3. Categorize into FAQ (Standard) and SHAQ (Deep Insight).
• Quality Check: Do queries have specific intent (commercial/transactional)?
• Failure Mode: Targeting generic "What is X" terms that have no conversion value.
MODULE 3: whole-solution-writer
• Purpose: Execution. Creates the DSA content.
• Workflow:
    1. Input Module 2 Research + Brand Knowledge Base.
    2. Draft using 9-Block DSA Framework.
    3. Add "Citation Snippets" (40-60 word factual blocks) for LLM pickup.
    4. Human Edit: Remove "AI accent," verify data, add internal links.
• Quality Check: Does it pass the "One Sentence Rule" (Full solution + why it works)?.
• Failure Mode: Publishing raw AI slop without human verification or unique data points.
MODULE 4: youtube-script-generator
• Purpose: Execution. Repurposing for the #2 Search Engine.
• Workflow:
    1. Ingest WSA (Module 3).
    2. Convert to Script: Hook -> Value -> Proof -> CTA.
    3. Generate YouTube Chapters (Timecodes) for key moments.
• Quality Check: Does the Hook interrupt the pattern? Are Chapters optimized for search queries?
• Failure Mode: "Reading the blog post" on camera. Must be conversational and visual.
MODULE 5: lead-magnet-creator
• Purpose: Execution. Capturing the traffic that remains.
• Workflow:
    1. Extract "Protocol" and "Stacks" from WSA.
    2. Format as PDF Checklist or Mini-Course.
    3. Create Landing Page copy with "Direct Response" tone.
• Quality Check: Does the Lead Magnet solve a specific, immediate problem?
• Failure Mode: Generic "Newsletters" or "Whitepapers" that nobody wants.
MODULE 6: geo-site-orchestrator
• Purpose: Infrastructure. Building the machine.
• Workflow:
    1. Implement Schema (Organization, Product, FAQ).
    2. Build "Contextual Bridges" (Internal links with "Why/Because" logic).
    3. Manage Programmatic Pages (e.g., "Competitor vs. Us" pages).
• Quality Check: Is Schema validating? Are internal links descriptive?
• Failure Mode: Orphan pages with no internal links; Broken Schema.
MODULE 7: ai-seo-site-auditor (The Discovery Closer)
• Purpose: Diagnosis. Selling the service.
• Workflow:
    1. BOFU Gap Analysis: What money keywords do competitors rank for that the client misses?.
    2. Topical Authority Map: Visualize the client's weak coverage vs. market leaders.
    3. Site Scorecard: Technical health + AI Readiness (Schema/Data).
• Quality Check: Does the audit lead directly to a revenue opportunity (not just "fix 404s")?
• Failure Mode: Overwhelming the client with technical jargon instead of revenue gaps.
