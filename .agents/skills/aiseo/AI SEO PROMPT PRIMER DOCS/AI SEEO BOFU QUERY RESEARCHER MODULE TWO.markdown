A. Category Map: The Intelligence Battlefield
Topic Cluster: High-Intent Query Discovery & Commercial Intent Analysis. Target Persona: The "Revenue-First" SEO Strategist.
1. The 5 Core Pains (Research Deep Dive)
Ģ Pain 1: The "Vanity Volume" Illusion
    ? Symptom: "We rank #1 for a keyword with 20,000 monthly searches, but we have zero conversions."
    ? Root Cause: The strategy targeted Informational (TOFU) queries like "What is SEO?" which AI Mode now answers instantly (Zero-Click). The user has no purchase intent.
    ? Strategic Implication: Volume is now a liability if it lacks intent. You must pivot to "Low Volume, High Value" queries where the user needs to verify data or compare options.
Ģ Pain 2: The "Hidden" Dark Funnel
    ? Symptom: "Customers say they found us on Reddit or ChatGPT, but our keyword tools show zero search volume for those terms."
    ? Root Cause: Traditional tools (Ahrefs/Semrush) rely on clickstream data that lags behind actual user behavior. They miss "Zero-Volume" queries that occur inside private communities or chat interfaces.
    ? Strategic Implication: You cannot rely solely on tools. You must mine "Pain Language" directly from the source (Reddit/forums) to find the queries tools haven't indexed yet.
Ģ Pain 3: The "Generic" Content Trap
    ? Symptom: "We wrote 50 articles, but they all sound like Wikipedia. Nobody cares."
    ? Root Cause: Research was based on "Keyword Difficulty" rather than "User Frustration." The content answers the "What" but misses the "So What?"
    ? Strategic Implication: Research must extract verbatim customer complaints ("I hate when X happens") to fuel content that resonates emotionally.
Ģ Pain 4: AI Displacement Anxiety
    ? Symptom: "Google's AI Overview is sitting on top of my best keyword. My traffic is gone."
    ? Root Cause: The query was simple enough for an LLM to summarize. You targeted a "Definition" rather than a "Decision."
    ? Strategic Implication: You must identify "AI Risk" before writing. If an AI can answer it in 2 sentences, don't write an article. Target complex decisions ("X vs Y for [Specific Use Case]").
Ģ Pain 5: Competitor Blindness
    ? Symptom: "Competitors are winning deals we didn't even know existed."
    ? Root Cause: Competitors are targeting adjacent niches or specific problem-solution queries you ignored because they looked "too small."
    ? Strategic Implication: Gap analysis must look for "Money Keywords" (pricing, vs, best) specifically, not just broad topic gaps.

B. FAQ ? SHAQ Ladder (Research Focused)
FAQ (The Symptom)
SHAQ (The Mechanism)
Deep Strategic Insight & Citation
"What keywords have the highest volume?"
"Which queries indicate the user is holding their credit card?"
Mechanism: High volume often correlates with low intent. AI Mode absorbs volume. Strategy: Prioritize "Commercial Intent" (best, vs, pricing) over volume. A keyword with 50 searches/mo and high intent is worth more than 5,000 informational searches.
"Should I target keywords with KD 0?"
"Does this low-competition keyword actually solve a problem my product fixes?"
Mechanism: "Easy" keywords are often irrelevant. Strategy: Use the MMA Framework (Money, Movement, Authority). Don't rank for things that don't drive revenue just because it's easy.
"How do I find what to write about?"
"What are the specific, verbatim complaints my customers are posting on Reddit?"
Mechanism: Reddit/Quora contain the "raw data" of user desire before it becomes a keyword. Strategy: Mine forums for "I hate X" or "How do I fix Y" to find angles competitors miss.
"Why isn't my 'Ultimate Guide' ranking?"
"Did I target a query where the user wants a quick answer, or a deep solution?"
Mechanism: User intent mismatch. If they want a definition, they won't read a 3,000-word guide. Strategy: Map content format (Video, Tool, Guide) to the specific intent of the query.
"Can I use AI to find keywords?"
"How do I use AI to simulate the 'Query Fan-out' behavior of a buying agent?"
Mechanism: AI agents search differently than humans (long-tail, multi-step). Strategy: Use Perplexity to simulate how an agent researches a topic to find the "hidden" questions you need to answer.

C. HCTS Library (Research Tactics)
HACKS (Fast Wins)
1. The "GSC Regex" Goldmine: Use Regular Expressions in Google Search Console to instantly filter your existing data for commercial intent. Pattern: (best|review|vs|pricing|alternative). This reveals money keywords you already rank for but haven't optimized.
2. The "Reddit Site: Search": Use Google to search site:reddit.com [niche] "frustrated" or site:reddit.com [niche] "waste of money". This exposes the exact pain points users are desperate to solve.
3. The "Perplexity Mirror": Ask Perplexity "What are the top 10 questions high-net-worth individuals ask about [Topic]?". This reveals the specific concerns of your most valuable buyers, often missed by generic tools.
CRACKS (Failure Points)
1. The "Zero-Click" Blindspot: Targeting "What is [X]" keywords without checking the SERP. If an AI Overview appears and answers it perfectly, you will get 0 clicks. You must verify "AI Risk" before investing.
2. The "Keyword Stuffing" Relic: Creating separate pages for "SEO Agency," "SEO Firm," and "SEO Services." Google treats these as the same intent. You will cannibalize your own rankings. Cluster them into one "Hub" page.
3. The "Volume Vanity": Ignoring long-tail queries (4+ words) because tools say "0-10 volume." These queries often convert at 20%+ because the intent is so specific.
TRACKS (Paths to Success)
1. The "Competitor Gap" Sprint: Export competitor keywords -> Filter for "Commercial Intent" -> Remove keywords you already rank for -> Sort by KD (Keyword Difficulty). This creates an immediate "Hit List" of high-value targets.
2. The "Pain-to-Query" Translation: Take a Reddit complaint ("I can't find a CRM that handles invoices") and translate it into a search query ("CRM with invoicing features"). This targets the solution to a validated problem.
STACKS (Tool Combos)
1. The "Intent Filter" Stack: Ahrefs (Export) + GSC (Validation) + Regex Filter (Sorting).
2. The "Agent Simulation" Stack: Perplexity (Brainstorming) + SE Ranking (SERP Feature Tracking) + AlsoAsked.com (Question mapping).

MODULE 2 BLUEPRINT: bofu-query-researcher
1. Module Purpose & Context
Role: The "Intelligence Officer." It translates the broad strategy from Module 1 into specific, actionable targets. Why it exists: Most content fails because it targets queries users ask, not queries users buy from. This module filters out the noise to find the signal. Key Output: A prioritized BOFU Keyword Master List (50-100 queries) with intent classification, pain point mapping, and AI risk assessment.
2. Inputs Required
Ģ From Module 1: Platform Priority List, Topic Cluster Map, Initial Seed Keywords.
Ģ Competitor URLs: 3-5 direct competitors (from Strategy phase).
Ģ Product/Service DNA: What is the specific mechanism/offer? (e.g., "AI SEO Software," not just "SEO Tool").
Ģ Persona Data: Job title, primary frustration, budget level.
3. Step-by-Step Workflow
Phase 1: Seed Keyword Expansion (Days 1-2)
Ģ Step 1.1: Load Seed List from Module 1.
Ģ Step 1.2: Use Ahrefs "Matching Terms" or Semrush "Keyword Magic Tool."
    ? Action: Enter seed (e.g., "Project Management"). Select "Questions" and "Phrase Match."
Ģ Step 1.3: Apply "Commercial Modifiers" immediately.
    ? Modifiers: Best, Vs, Review, Alternative, Pricing, Cost, Demo, Software, Tool, Agency, Service.
Ģ Output: A raw list of 200-300 commercially-slanted keywords.
Phase 2: Commercial Intent Filtering (Days 2-3)
Ģ Step 2.1: Apply the p_bofu_regex_filter.
    ? Regex: (best|review|vs|alternative|pricing|cost|comparison|demo|audit|software|tool|service|hire).
Ģ Step 2.2: The "Zero-Click" Purge.
    ? Action: Delete any query that is purely definitional ("What is", "Define", "History of"). AI Mode eats these.
Ģ Step 2.3: "Wallet Proximity" Scoring.
    ? Score 1-3: Informational (Delete).
    ? Score 4-6: Consideration (Keep for retargeting).
    ? Score 7-10: Transactional (Prioritize).
Ģ Output: A filtered list of 100-150 High-Intent keywords.
Phase 3: AI Mode Risk Assessment (Day 3)
Ģ Step 3.1: Manual SERP Check.
    ? Action: Google the top 20 keywords.
Ģ Step 3.2: Feature Identification.
    ? Check: Does an AI Overview appear? Does a Featured Snippet answer the question 100%?
    ? Tag: If YES -> Tag as "High AI Risk" (Requires Schema/Data Table strategy).
    ? Tag: If NO -> Tag as "Standard SEO" (Blue link opportunity).
Ģ Output: AI Risk column populated in Master List.
Phase 4: Pain Point Mining (Days 4-5)
Ģ Step 4.1: The "Reddit/Quora" Deep Dive.
    ? Action: Use Google: site:reddit.com [Keyword] ("frustrated" OR "hate" OR "fail" OR "slow").
Ģ Step 4.2: Verbatim Extraction.
    ? Action: Copy the exact sentence the user wrote. (e.g., "Why does every CRM cost $50/mo just for email?").
Ģ Step 4.3: Map Pain to Keyword.
    ? Link: "CRM cost complaint" -> Keyword: "Affordable CRM for small business."
Ģ Output: Pain Point Library (20-30 verbatim entries linked to keywords).
Phase 5: Competitive Gap Analysis (Day 5-6)
Ģ Step 5.1: Competitor Export.
    ? Action: Put Competitor URL into Ahrefs/Semrush. Filter by "Top Pages."
Ģ Step 5.2: The "Missing Branch" Check.
    ? Analysis: What topics do they cover that we have zero content for? (e.g., They have a "Templates" hub, we don't).
Ģ Step 5.3: "Weak Spot" Identification.
    ? Analysis: Where do they rank on Page 2 (Positions 11-20)? These are vulnerabilities we can exploit with better content.
Ģ Output: A list of "Must-Build" keywords based on competitor gaps.
Phase 6: Prioritization & Roadmap (Day 6-7)
Ģ Step 6.1: MMA Scoring.
    ? Money (1-10): Revenue potential.
    ? Movement (1-10): Speed to rank (KD < 20 = High Movement).
    ? Authority (1-10): Strategic importance to brand.
Ģ Step 6.2: Final Sort.
    ? Logic: Sort by Total Score. The top 20 are your "Phase 1" content plan.
Ģ Step 6.3: Asset Mapping.
    ? Action: Assign a Content Type to each keyword (WSA Article, YouTube Video, Lead Magnet).
Ģ Output: Finalized BOFU Keyword Master List.
4. Quality Checks (The "Gate")
Ģ [ ] The Wallet Test: Do the keywords imply the user is ready to buy?
Ģ [ ] The Zero-Click Check: Did we remove queries AI answers instantly?
Ģ [ ] The Pain Check: Do we have verbatim customer quotes for every major keyword cluster?
Ģ [ ] The Platform Fit: Are video-friendly keywords mapped to Module 4 (YouTube)?
5. Failure Modes
Ģ Failure Mode 1: The "Vanity Metric" Trap.
    ? Symptom: Targeting "SEO" (50k vol) instead of "SaaS SEO Agency" (200 vol).
    ? Fix: Delete high-volume informational terms.
Ģ Failure Mode 2: "Keyword Cannibalization."
    ? Symptom: Targeting "Best CRM" and "Top CRM" with two different pages.
    ? Fix: Cluster semantic variations into ONE url.
Ģ Failure Mode 3: Ignoring AI Risk.
    ? Symptom: Writing a simple definition post that gets crushed by an AI Overview.
    ? Fix: Ensure every topic requires depth, opinion, or data AI cannot fake.
