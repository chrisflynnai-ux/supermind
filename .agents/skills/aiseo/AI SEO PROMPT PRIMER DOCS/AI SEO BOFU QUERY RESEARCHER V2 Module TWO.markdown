1. GEO Knowledge Compendium: Research/Discovery Layer
A. Category Map: The Intelligence Battlefield
Topic Cluster: High-Intent Query Discovery & Commercial Intent Analysis. Target Persona: The "Revenue-First" SEO Strategist who refuses to chase vanity metrics.
1. The 5 Core Pains (Research Deep Dive)
• Pain 1: The "Vanity Volume" Illusion
    ? Symptom: "We rank #1 for a keyword with 20,000 monthly searches, but we have zero conversions."
    ? Root Cause: The strategy targeted Informational (TOFU) queries like "What is SEO?" which AI Mode now answers instantly (Zero-Click). The user has no purchase intent.
    ? Strategic Implication: Volume is now a liability if it lacks intent. You must pivot to "Low Volume, High Value" queries where the user needs to verify data or compare options.
    ? Evidence: "Clicks to customer websites are decreasing because Google is now using AI to show AI-created summaries".
• Pain 2: The "Hidden" Dark Funnel
    ? Symptom: "Customers say they found us on Reddit or ChatGPT, but our keyword tools show zero search volume for those terms."
    ? Root Cause: Traditional tools (Ahrefs/Semrush) rely on clickstream data that lags behind actual user behavior. They miss "Zero-Volume" queries that occur inside private communities or chat interfaces.
    ? Strategic Implication: You cannot rely solely on tools. You must mine "Pain Language" directly from the source (Reddit/forums) to find the queries tools haven't indexed yet.
    ? Evidence: "Reddit threads appear in 82% of Google searches on page one... most people have no idea this is happening".
• Pain 3: The "Generic" Content Trap
    ? Symptom: "We wrote 50 articles, but they all sound like Wikipedia. Nobody cares."
    ? Root Cause: Research was based on "Keyword Difficulty" rather than "User Frustration." The content answers the "What" but misses the "So What?"
    ? Strategic Implication: Research must extract verbatim customer complaints ("I hate when X happens") to fuel content that resonates emotionally.
• Pain 4: AI Displacement Anxiety
    ? Symptom: "Google's AI Overview is sitting on top of my best keyword. My traffic is gone."
    ? Root Cause: The query was simple enough for an LLM to summarize. You targeted a "Definition" rather than a "Decision."
    ? Strategic Implication: You must identify "AI Risk" before writing. If an AI can answer it in 2 sentences, don't write an article. Target complex decisions ("X vs Y for [Specific Use Case]").
    ? Evidence: "If an AI overview is present on the search results page, people are about half as likely to click through".
• Pain 5: Competitor Blindness
    ? Symptom: "Competitors are winning deals we didn't even know existed."
    ? Root Cause: Competitors are targeting adjacent niches or specific problem-solution queries you ignored because they looked "too small."
    ? Strategic Implication: Gap analysis must look for "Money Keywords" (pricing, vs, best) specifically, not just broad topic gaps.

"Does this low-competition keyword actually solve a problem my product fixes?"
Mechanism: "Easy" keywords are often irrelevant. Strategy: Use the MMA Framework (Money, Movement, Authority). Don't rank for things that don't drive revenue just because it's easy.

"What are the specific, verbatim complaints my customers are posting on Reddit?"
Mechanism: Reddit/Quora contain the "raw data" of user desire before it becomes a keyword. Strategy: Mine forums for "I hate X" or "How do I fix Y" to find angles competitors miss.
"Why isn't my 'Ultimate Guide' ranking?"
"Did I target a query where the user wants a quick answer, or a deep solution?"
Mechanism: User intent mismatch. If they want a definition, they won't read a 3,000-word guide. Strategy: Map content format (Video, Tool, Guide) to the specific intent of the query.

"How do I use AI to simulate the 'Query Fan-out' behavior of a buying agent?"
Mechanism: AI agents search differently than humans (long-tail, multi-step). Strategy: Use Perplexity to simulate how an agent researches a topic to find the "hidden" questions you need to answer.

C. HCTS Library (Research Tactics)
HACKS (Fast Wins)
1. The "GSC Regex" Goldmine: Use Regular Expressions in Google Search Console to instantly filter your existing data for commercial intent. Pattern: (best|review|vs|pricing|alternative). This reveals money keywords you already rank for but haven't optimized.
2. The "Reddit Site: Search": Use Google to search site:reddit.com [niche] "frustrated" or site:reddit.com [niche] "waste of money". This exposes the exact pain points users are desperate to solve.
3. The "Perplexity Mirror": Ask Perplexity "What are the top 10 questions high-net-worth individuals ask about [Topic]?". This reveals the specific concerns of your most valuable buyers, often missed by generic tools.
CRACKS (Failure Points)
1. The "Zero-Click" Blindspot: Targeting "What is [X]" keywords without checking the SERP. If an AI Overview appears and answers it perfectly, you will get 0 clicks. You must verify "AI Risk" before investing.
2. The "Keyword Stuffing" Relic: Creating separate pages for "SEO Agency," "SEO Firm," and "SEO Services." Google treats these as the same intent. You will cannibalize your own rankings. Cluster them into one "Hub" page.
3. The "Volume Vanity": Ignoring long-tail queries (4+ words) because tools say "0-10 volume." These queries often convert at 20%+ because the intent is so specific.
TRACKS (Paths to Success)
1. The "Competitor Gap" Sprint: Export competitor keywords -> Filter for "Commercial Intent" -> Remove keywords you already rank for -> Sort by KD (Keyword Difficulty). This creates an immediate "Hit List" of high-value targets.
2. The "Pain-to-Query" Translation: Take a Reddit complaint ("I can't find a CRM that handles invoices") and translate it into a search query ("CRM with invoicing features"). This targets the solution to a validated problem.
STACKS (Tool Combinations)
1. The "Intent Filter" Stack: Ahrefs (Export) + GSC (Validation) + Regex Filter (Sorting).
2. The "Agent Simulation" Stack: Perplexity (Brainstorming) + SE Ranking (SERP Feature Tracking) + AlsoAsked.com (Question mapping).

2. Module 2 Blueprint: bofu-query-researcher
A. Purpose & Strategic Context
Role: The "Intelligence Officer." It translates the broad strategy from Module 1 into specific, actionable targets. Why it exists: Most content fails because it targets queries users ask, not queries users buy from. In the AI era, informational queries are dead ends. This module filters out the noise to find the "Money Keywords." Positioning: This module receives strategic direction from Module 1 (Strategy) and feeds validated topics to Module 3 (Writer) and Module 4 (YouTube). Key Output: A prioritized BOFU Keyword Master List (50-100 queries) with intent classification, pain point mapping, and AI risk assessment.
B. Inputs Required
To run this module effectively, the agent (or human) requires:
1. From Module 1: Platform Priority List, Topic Cluster Map, Initial Seed Keywords (from Strategy phase).
2. Competitor URLs: 3-5 direct competitors (identified in Module 1).
3. Product/Service DNA: What is the specific mechanism/offer? (e.g., "AI SEO Software," not just "SEO Tool").
4. Persona Data: Job title, primary frustration, budget level.
5. Tools Required: Ahrefs/Semrush (Data), Google Search Console (Validation), Perplexity (Intent Analysis).
C. Outputs Produced
1. BOFU Keyword Master List: 50-100 high-intent queries filtered by "Wallet Proximity" (fed to Module 3).
    ? Columns: Keyword, Search Vol, KD, Intent Score (1-10), AI Risk (High/Low).
2. Pain Point Library: 20-30 verbatim user questions from Reddit/Quora that expose real frustrations (fed to Module 3 for hooks).
3. Competitive Gap Report: A list of "Money Keywords" competitors rank for that we miss.
4. Intent Classification Map: Grouping keywords by buyer stage (Problem Aware vs. Purchase Ready).
D. Step-by-Step Workflow (Detailed)
Phase 1: Seed Keyword Expansion (Day 1 - 3 Hours)
• Step 1.1: Load Seed List from Module 1.
    ? Action: Retrieve the "Money Topics" defined in the strategy layer.
    ? Example: "AI SEO Tools," "SEO Agency Pricing."
• Step 1.2: Use Keyword Tool "Matching Terms" (Ahrefs/Semrush).
    ? Action: Enter seed. Select "Matching Terms."
    ? Filter: Include "Questions" or "Phrase Match."
• Step 1.3: Apply "Commercial Modifiers" immediately.
    ? Action: Filter the raw list using these modifiers: best, vs, review, alternative, pricing, cost, demo, software, tool, agency, service.
    ? Output: A raw list of 200-300 commercially-slanted keywords.
Phase 2: Commercial Intent Filtering (Day 1 Afternoon - 3 Hours)
• Step 2.1: Apply the p_bofu_regex_filter.
    ? Regex: (best|review|vs|alternative|pricing|cost|comparison|demo|audit|software|tool|service|hire).
• Step 2.2: The "Zero-Click" Purge.
    ? Action: Delete any query that is purely definitional ("What is", "Define", "History of"). AI Mode eats these.
• Step 2.3: "Wallet Proximity" Scoring.
    ? Score 1-3: Informational (Delete).
    ? Score 4-6: Consideration (Keep for retargeting/nurture).
    ? Score 7-10: Transactional (Prioritize).
    ? Output: A filtered list of 100-150 High-Intent keywords.
Phase 3: AI Mode Risk Assessment (Day 2 Morning - 2 Hours)
• Step 3.1: Manual SERP Check.
    ? Action: Google the top 20 keywords from your list.
• Step 3.2: Feature Identification.
    ? Check: Does an AI Overview appear? Does a Featured Snippet answer the question 100%?
    ? Decision:
        ? If YES (AI Overview dominates): Tag as "High AI Risk." Strategy must shift to "Citation Optimization" (Data tables/Schema).
        ? If NO (Blue Links visible): Tag as "Standard SEO" (Blue link opportunity).
    ? Output: AI Risk column populated in Master List.
Phase 4: Pain Point Mining (Day 2 Afternoon - 3 Hours)
• Step 4.1: The "Reddit/Quora" Deep Dive.
    ? Action: Use Google: site:reddit.com [Keyword] ("frustrated" OR "hate" OR "fail" OR "slow").
• Step 4.2: Verbatim Extraction.
    ? Action: Copy the exact sentence the user wrote. (e.g., "Why does every CRM cost $50/mo just for email?").
• Step 4.3: Map Pain to Keyword.
    ? Link: "CRM cost complaint" -> Keyword: "Affordable CRM for small business."
    ? Output: Pain Point Library (20-30 verbatim entries).
Phase 5: Competitive Gap Analysis (Day 3 - 3 Hours)
• Step 5.1: Competitor Export.
    ? Action: Put Competitor URL into Ahrefs/Semrush. Filter by "Top Pages."
• Step 5.2: The "Missing Branch" Check.
    ? Analysis: What topics do they cover that we have zero content for? (e.g., They have a "Templates" hub, we don't).
• Step 5.3: "Weak Spot" Identification.
    ? Analysis: Where do they rank on Page 2 (Positions 11-20)? These are vulnerabilities we can exploit with better content.
    ? Output: A list of "Must-Build" keywords based on competitor gaps.
Phase 6: Prioritization & Roadmap (Day 3 Afternoon - 2 Hours)
• Step 6.1: MMA Scoring.
    ? Money (1-10): Revenue potential.
    ? Movement (1-10): Speed to rank (KD < 20 = High Movement).
    ? Authority (1-10): Strategic importance to brand.
• Step 6.2: Final Sort.
    ? Logic: Sort by Total Score. The top 20 are your "Phase 1" content plan.
• Step 6.3: Asset Mapping.
    ? Action: Assign a Content Type to each keyword (WSA Article, YouTube Video, Lead Magnet).
    ? Output: Finalized BOFU Keyword Master List.
E. Quality Checks (The "Gate")
• [ ] The Wallet Test: Do the keywords imply the user is ready to buy?
• [ ] The Zero-Click Check: Did we remove queries AI answers instantly?
• [ ] The Pain Check: Do we have verbatim customer quotes for every major keyword cluster?

F. Failure Modes (What to Avoid)
Failure Mode 1: The "Vanity Metric" Trap
• Symptom: Targeting "SEO" (50k vol) instead of "SaaS SEO Agency" (200 vol).
• Root Cause: Traditional SEO mindset prioritizing search volume over commercial intent.
• Diagnostic: Does the keyword list contain general terms without modifiers?
• Fix: Apply the p_bofu_regex_filter to kill informational noise. Delete any keyword scoring <4/10 on the Wallet Proximity scale.
Failure Mode 2: "Keyword Cannibalization"
• Symptom: Targeting "Best CRM" and "Top CRM" with two different pages.
• Root Cause: Misunderstanding semantic clustering.
• Diagnostic: Do you have multiple keywords with the exact same search intent mapped to different URLs?
• Fix: Cluster semantically identical keywords into ONE target URL. Use variations naturally in the content.
Failure Mode 3: Ignoring AI Risk
• Symptom: Writing a simple definition post that gets crushed by an AI Overview.
• Root Cause: Failing to check the SERP for AI domination.
• Diagnostic: Google the target keyword. Is the answer provided in the snapshot?
• Fix: Ensure every topic requires depth, opinion, or data AI cannot fake.
Failure Mode 4: The "Zero-Volume" Panic
• Symptom: Avoiding long-tail keywords because Ahrefs shows "0-10 volume."
• Root Cause: Trusting tool data over user behavior.
• Diagnostic: Are you deleting keywords that represent real pains just because tools show N/A volume?
• Fix: Trust Pain Point Mining (Phase 4). If users are complaining about it on Reddit, the search volume exists, even if tools don't see it yet.

3. Knowledge Blocks (Deep Dive Frameworks)
KB-BOFU-QUERY-INTELLIGENCE-v1.0 (5-6 Pages)
1. Commercial Intent Taxonomy
To accurately identify "Money Keywords," you must look beyond basic modifiers. Use this 12+ modifier taxonomy to filter for intent.
• Comparison Modifiers: vs, versus, alternative, compared to, instead of, competitor.
    ? Intent: The user has narrowed options and is making a final choice.
• Evaluation Modifiers: review, rating, pros and cons, worth it, legit, scam, case study.
    ? Intent: The user needs validation before purchase.
• Purchase Modifiers: pricing, cost, demo, trial, buy, hire, agency, service, consultant.
    ? Intent: The user has their wallet out.
• Implementation Modifiers: how to use, setup, install, integrate, configure.
    ? Intent: Post-purchase or pre-purchase technical validation (High retention value).
• Problem-Solving Modifiers: fix, troubleshoot, not working, error, issue, slow.
    ? Intent: High urgency. If you solve the pain, you win the trust,.
2. Buyer Stage Mapping
Keywords map directly to the psychological state of the buyer.
• Stage 1: Problem Aware: "How to [solve X]".
    ? Example: "How to fix low organic traffic."
    ? Action: Nurture with educational content.
• Stage 2: Solution Aware: "Best [category] for [use case]".
    ? Example: "Best SEO agency for SaaS."
    ? Action: Comparison guides, "Best of" lists.
• Stage 3: Product Aware: "[Brand A] vs [Brand B]".
    ? Example: "Rankability vs Ahrefs."
    ? Action: Direct comparison pages, "Why Us" pages.
• Stage 4: Purchase Ready: "Pricing," "Demo," "Audit".
    ? Example: "Rankability pricing," "Book SEO audit."
    ? Action: Sales pages, checkout flows.
3. AI Mode Risk Indicators
Not all keywords are safe to target. AI Overviews cannibalize clicks for specific types of queries.
• High AI Risk (Avoid/Pivot):
    ? Definitional: "What is programmatic SEO?" (AI answers 95% of time).
    ? Simple How-To: "How to tie a tie." (AI answers 80% of time).
    ? Calculations: "15% of 200." (AI answers 100% of time).
    ? Listicles: "Top 10 movies 2024." (AI generates list 70% of time).
• Low AI Risk (Target):
    ? Complex Comparisons: "Ahrefs vs Semrush for enterprise agencies." (AI struggles with nuance).
    ? Niche Long-Tail: "Best CRM for plumbing businesses in Texas." (Data scarcity prevents AI hallucinations).
    ? Opinion/Experience: "Is SEO worth it in 2026?" (Requires human perspective).
    ? Action: When you see High AI Risk, do not write a standard blog post. Create a tool, a video, or a deep data study.
4. Query Intent Scoring Rubric (1-10)
Use this scale to score every keyword in your Master List.
• Score 1: Pure navigation ("Facebook login"). Action: Delete.
• Score 2-3: Basic information ("What is SEO definition"). Action: Delete.
• Score 4-5: Educational ("SEO tips for beginners"). Action: Keep for social/nurture.
• Score 6-7: Consideration ("Best SEO tools comparison"). Action: Priority Level 2 (WSA).
• Score 8-9: Evaluation ("Ahrefs vs Semrush for agencies"). Action: Priority Level 1 (Comparison Page).
• Score 10: Transactional ("Buy Ahrefs subscription," "SEO Agency Pricing"). Action: Priority Level 1 (Money Page).

Pain points are the raw material for high-converting content. You must find them where users vent.
• Reddit: Use search operators to find threads where users are angry or confused.
    ? Operator: site:reddit.com [keyword] "frustrated" OR "problem" OR "doesn't work".
    ? Action: Look for comments with high upvotes that express a shared struggle.

    ? Action: Look for questions with many followers but few good answers.

    ? Operator: [keyword] min_faves:50.
    ? Action: Find tweets complaining about a competitor or a process.

    ? Action: Extract common themes. "Too expensive," "Bad support," "Hard to use.".

When you find a pain point, tag it to make it actionable.
• Dimension 1: Persona. Who is complaining?
    ? Examples: "Frustrated SaaS Founder," "Overwhelmed Marketing Manager."
• Dimension 2: Emotion. What are they feeling?
    ? Examples: "Anger at agencies," "Fear of wasting money," "Skepticism about results."
• Dimension 3: Stage. Where are they in the funnel?
    ? Examples: "Unaware" (doesn't know solution), "Problem Aware" (knows pain, needs fix).

KB-COMPETITIVE-INTELLIGENCE-v1.0 (4-5 Pages)
1. Gap Analysis Framework (4 Types)
Don't just look for keywords. Look for strategic gaps.
• Content Gaps: Topics they cover that we don't.
    ? Fix: Build pages for these topics immediately.
• Keyword Gaps: Specific queries they rank for in positions 1-10 that we miss.
    ? Fix: Optimize existing content or build new pages.
• Authority Gaps: Backlinks or mentions they have that we don't.
    ? Fix: Targeted outreach or "Authority Hijacking" (Module 1).
• Platform Gaps: Channels they dominate (YouTube, LinkedIn) that we ignore.
    ? Fix: Launch content on those platforms.
2. "Steal and Improve" Strategy
How to beat a competitor who is already ranking.
• Step 1: Find their top-ranking page (Position 1-3).
• Step 2: Analyze WHY it ranks.
    ? Check: Backlinks, Content Depth, Schema, Video Embeds, Data Tables.
• Step 3: Build a better version.
    ? Improve: Add 20% more depth (not fluff). Add unique data/charts. Add video. Update the information to 2026. Implement better Schema.
• Step 4: "The Pivot."
    ? Tactic: Identify their weakness (e.g., "They don't cover AI Mode"). Make that your strength. Explicitly mention what they missed.
3. Competitive Entity Analysis
Check if your competitor is a recognized "Entity" in the Knowledge Graph.
• Step 1: Search "[Competitor Name]" in Perplexity/Google.
• Step 2: Evaluate.
    ? Detailed Bio/Knowledge Panel: Strong Entity. Hard to beat on authority.
    ? No Info: Weak Entity. Vulnerable. You can beat them with better E-E-A-T signals.
• Step 3: Close the gap.
    ? Action: Implement Organization Schema, get press mentions, define your entity clearly on your About page.

KB-MMA-PRIORITIZATION-v1.0 (4-5 Pages)
1. The MMA Scoring Framework
A mathematical way to prioritize keywords.
• Money (1-10): Revenue potential. (Weighted x2).
    ? 1-3: Informational (No wallet).
    ? 7-10: Transactional (Wallet out).
• Movement (1-10): Speed to rank.
    ? 1-3: Dominated by giants (Ahrefs, HubSpot). Slow.
    ? 7-10: Low competition (Long-tail, new terms). Fast.
• Authority (1-10): Strategic value.
    ? 1-3: Tangential.
    ? 7-10: Core to brand mission.
• Formula: (Money x 2) + Movement + Authority = Total Score (Max 50).
2. The "Quick Win" Filter
Target these first to show results in <90 days.
• Criteria: Movement ? 7, Money ? 5.
• Why: These are low-competition, commercial queries. You can rank fast and generate revenue.
• Examples: "Technical SEO audit for SaaS" (Score 31/50). "Affordable CRM for small business" (Score 32/50).
3. The "Authority Play" Strategy
Target these for long-term dominance, even if ranking is slow.
• Criteria: Authority ? 8, Money ? 4.
• Why: You need these pages to be seen as an expert, even if you don't rank #1 immediately. They anchor your topic cluster.
• Examples: "SEO strategy guide" (Score 21/50). Hard to rank, but essential for the cluster.
