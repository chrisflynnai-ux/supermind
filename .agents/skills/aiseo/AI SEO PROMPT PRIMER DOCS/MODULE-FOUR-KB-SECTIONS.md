## KB-RETENTION-MECHANICS-v1.0 (5-6 Pages)

### 1. Script Workflow (Psychology → Packaging → Outline → Intro → Body → Outro)

**The Fatal Mistake:** Most creators write linearly (intro first). This creates weak hooks because you don't know where the video is going.

**The Correct Order:**

**Step 1: Psychology (30 minutes)**
- Define the EVR gap you're creating
- Identify the expectation viewers currently hold
- Craft the reality that will surprise them
- Score the gap (must be 7+)

**Step 2: Packaging (45 minutes)**
- Write title (3 variants)
- Design thumbnail concept (stun type selection)
- Write first spoken sentence
- Verify 3-Hook Alignment

**Step 3: Outline (60 minutes)**
- Define 5-7 main points
- Map to neuro-axis timeline
- Insert grease-slide connectors between points
- Run uniqueness gate check

**Step 4: Body First (2 hours)**
- Write main value content
- Include data tables, examples, case snippets
- Add B-roll and visual cues
- Ensure rising pattern recognition

**Step 5: Intro Second (60 minutes)**
- Craft hook using EVR formula
- Add pattern interrupt
- Write thesis statement
- Promise specific outcome

**Step 6: Outro Last (45 minutes)**
- Write fortune cookie insight
- Integrate native CTA
- Add loop to next video
- Check total length vs neuro-timeline

### 2. Body Sequencing (Purchase Driver Timeline)

**The Principle:** BOFU videos must activate purchase drivers in psychological order.

**The 5-Stage Purchase Driver Timeline:**

**Stage 1: Problem Recognition (0-20% of video)**
- Neuro-Axis: BODY (safety threat) + HEART (emotional validation)
- Purchase Driver: "I have this problem and it matters"
- Content: Quantify the pain, show cost of inaction
- Example: "If your SaaS is stuck at $10K MRR after 18 months, the problem isn't your product—it's your SEO strategy. And it's costing you $40K-60K in MRR you should already have."

**Stage 2: Solution Awareness (20-40% of video)**
- Neuro-Axis: MIND (logical framework)
- Purchase Driver: "There is a solution and I understand how it works"
- Content: Introduce framework/methodology, explain mechanism
- Example: "The solution is called BOFU Targeting. Instead of chasing 50K monthly search keywords, you target 200 monthly search keywords with 10x higher purchase intent. Here's how it works..."

**Stage 3: Proof (40-60% of video)**
- Neuro-Axis: SPIRIT (social proof) + MIND (data validation)
- Purchase Driver: "This works for people like me"
- Content: Case studies, data tables, testimonials, before/after
- Example: "We tested this with 47 SaaS companies. The average result: 3.7x more trial signups with 40% less content production. Here's the breakdown..."

**Stage 4: Differentiation (60-80% of video)**
- Neuro-Axis: MIND (comparison) + PSYCHE (decision support)
- Purchase Driver: "This specific solution is better than alternatives"
- Content: Comparison to status quo or competitors
- Example: "Now you might be thinking: Can't I just use Ahrefs for this? Yes and no. Here's the difference..."

**Stage 5: Action Clarity (80-100% of video)**
- Neuro-Axis: PSYCHE (clear next step) + CONNECTION (join the winners)
- Purchase Driver: "I know exactly what to do next"
- Content: Implementation steps, native CTA, resource offer
- Example: "Here's exactly what to do: Step 1 - Download the BOFU keyword filter (link below). Step 2 - Run it on your current keyword list. Step 3 - Pick your top 10 highest-intent keywords..."

### 3. Grease-Slide Connectors (20+ Transition Phrases)

**The Problem:** Retention cliffs happen at topic transitions when viewers perceive a "chapter end."

**The Solution:** Use pattern-interrupt phrases that prevent the brain from detecting the transition.

**The 20+ Grease-Slide Connector Library:**

**Category 1: Escalation Connectors (makes it worse)**
1. "But it gets worse..."
2. "And here's the kicker..."
3. "Now multiply that by..."
4. "The real damage happens when..."

**Category 2: Hidden Truth Connectors (insider knowledge)**
5. "Here's the part no one tells you..."
6. "What they don't show you is..."
7. "The hidden cost is..."
8. "Behind the scenes, what's actually happening is..."

**Category 3: Perspective Shift Connectors (flip the frame)**
9. "Now flip that on its head..."
10. "But what if I told you..."
11. "Here's where it gets interesting..."
12. "This is where most people get it backwards..."

**Category 4: Curiosity Connectors (open loop)**
13. "Which brings us to the question..."
14. "That's when I discovered..."
15. "Here's what happened next..."
16. "And that's not even the crazy part..."

**Category 5: Validation Connectors (you're not alone)**
17. "I know what you're thinking..."
18. "This sounds crazy, but..."
19. "You're probably wondering..."
20. "Most people assume..."

**Usage Rules:**
- Insert every 90-120 seconds (measure via YouTube retention graphs)
- Vary the category (don't use 3 escalation connectors in a row)
- Pair with visual change (B-roll, zoom, new on-screen element)
- Test which connectors work best for your audience by checking retention graphs

**Example Script Segment with Connectors:**

> "So we targeted 12 BOFU keywords instead of 200 TOFU keywords. The result: 34 trial signups in the first month.
>
> [Grease-Slide: "But here's the part that surprised me..."]
>
> The keywords with the LOWEST search volume converted the highest. One keyword with only 40 monthly searches drove 7 trials. That's a 17.5% conversion rate.
>
> [Grease-Slide: "Which brings us to the question everyone asks..."]
>
> 'How do you find these keywords?' I'm going to show you the exact 3-step process. But first, you need to understand why traditional keyword research tools miss them..."

### 4. Rehook Strategy (Every 90-120 Seconds)

**The Attention Decay Curve:**
- At 0 seconds: 100% of viewers are paying attention
- At 30 seconds: 70-85% still engaged (if hook is strong)
- At 90 seconds: 50-60% start to drift
- At 180 seconds: 30-40% are thinking about leaving

**The Rehook Solution:** Every 90-120 seconds, create a micro-hook that renews the attention contract.

**Rehook Formula:**
```
[Connector Phrase] + [New Surprising Fact] + [Promise of What's Coming]

Example:
"But here's the part that shocked me [Connector] —
the #1 ranking keyword drove ZERO trials [Surprising Fact],
and I'm about to show you why that's actually good news [Promise]."
```

**Rehook Types:**

**Type 1: Data Surprise**
- "Here's what the data showed that I didn't expect..."
- Use when introducing new data point

**Type 2: Misconception Flip**
- "Most people think X, but the opposite is true..."
- Use when correcting common belief

**Type 3: Personal Story**
- "This is where I made a $12K mistake..."
- Use when sharing failure or lesson

**Type 4: Tease Next Insight**
- "That was just the setup. Wait until you see what happens when..."
- Use before major value drop

**Type 5: Audience Mind-Read**
- "I know you're thinking: 'This won't work for me because...' Let me address that..."
- Use when objection is likely

**Rehook Timing Map:**
```
0:00 - Primary Hook
1:30 - Rehook #1 (Connector + Data Surprise)
3:00 - Rehook #2 (Misconception Flip)
4:30 - Rehook #3 (Personal Story)
6:00 - Rehook #4 (Tease Next Insight)
7:30 - Rehook #5 (Audience Mind-Read)
9:00 - Final Hook (Fortune Cookie + CTA)
```

### 5. Visual Retention Aids

**The Multi-Sensory Engagement Principle:** Humans process visual information 60,000x faster than text. Retention improves when multiple senses are engaged simultaneously.

**Visual Retention Aid Types:**

**1. B-Roll Timing**
- **Rule:** Change visual every 5-10 seconds
- **Why:** Prevents "static screen fatigue"
- **BOFU Application:** Show product interfaces, data dashboards, real results
- **Example:** While explaining keyword research, show: Tool interface (5 sec) → Keyword list (5 sec) → Analytics graph (5 sec) → Face cam reaction (5 sec)

**2. On-Screen Text**
- **Rule:** Reinforce key numbers and phrases
- **Why:** 85% of social video is watched without sound (even on YouTube mobile)
- **BOFU Application:** Display prices, feature names, results
- **Example:** When saying "34 trial signups," display "34 TRIAL SIGNUPS" in bold on screen

**3. Data Visualization**
- **Rule:** Show, don't just tell numbers
- **Why:** Data tables are citation magnets for AI + improve human comprehension
- **BOFU Application:** Before/after comparisons, pricing tables, feature matrices
- **Example:** Create simple comparison table showing Old Strategy vs. New Strategy with highlighted winning metrics

**4. Pattern Interrupts**
- **Rule:** Use visual/audio "resets" every 45-60 seconds
- **Why:** Resets dopamine baseline, prevents habituation
- **Types:**
  - Sound effect (whoosh, ding, pop)
  - Zoom in/out
  - Color flash
  - Face cam ↔ Screen share switch
- **Example Timeline:**
  - 0:45 - Whoosh sound + zoom to data table
  - 1:30 - Switch from screen to face cam
  - 2:15 - Pop sound + highlight text
  - 3:00 - Zoom out to full screen

**5. Thumbnail Callbacks**
- **Rule:** Show thumbnail image in video at 0:03-0:05
- **Why:** Confirms the click, builds trust
- **BOFU Application:** If thumbnail shows "$10K MRR" graphic, display it on screen in intro
- **Example:** "See this? [Point to $10K MRR graphic on screen] This is exactly what I hit last month using the strategy I'm about to show you."

### 6. Balance Checking for Video (Excitatory ↔ Inhibitory Pairing)

**The Neuro-Axis Balance Principle:** Videos that over-activate excitatory axes (HEART, PSYCHE) without inhibitory balance (BODY, MIND) feel "salesy." Videos that over-activate inhibitory axes feel "boring."

**The 6 Neuro-Axes in Video:**

| Axis | Type | Video Activation Method | BOFU Application |
|------|------|------------------------|------------------|
| BODY (Safety) | Inhibitory | Show stability, reduce risk | "This is easy/safe to implement" |
| HEART (Emotion) | Excitatory | Agitate pain, show transformation | "Your competitors are winning while you struggle" |
| MIND (Logic) | Inhibitory | Data, frameworks, rational proof | "Here's the exact data showing..." |
| SPIRIT (Social) | Mixed | Show community, proof, belonging | "Here's what 47 SaaS founders discovered..." |
| PSYCHE (Action) | Excitatory | Clear steps, urgency, FOMO | "Do this now before..." |
| CONNECTION (Belong) | Mixed | Identity, tribe, insider knowledge | "If you're a SaaS founder..." |

**Balance Scoring System:**

**Step 1: Count Axis Activations by Minute**
- Watch your video
- Mark which axis is activated each minute
- Tally the count

**Example for 10-minute video:**
```
BODY: |||| (4 activations)
HEART: ||||| || (7 activations)
MIND: ||||| (5 activations)
SPIRIT: ||| (3 activations)
PSYCHE: ||||| | (6 activations)
CONNECTION: || (2 activations)
```

**Step 2: Check Excitatory/Inhibitory Balance**
- Excitatory Total: HEART (7) + PSYCHE (6) = 13
- Inhibitory Total: BODY (4) + MIND (5) = 9
- Difference: 13 - 9 = 4 points

**Balance Rules:**
- **Target Difference:** ≤4 points (balanced)
- **5-7 points:** Slightly salesy/boring (acceptable for some contexts)
- **8+ points:** Fix required (will feel off to viewers)

**Correction Strategies:**

**If Too Excitatory (overly emotional/urgent):**
- Add data visualization (MIND axis)
- Add "this is safe/easy" statements (BODY axis)
- Slow down pacing
- Example: "I know this sounds intense, but the implementation is actually straightforward. Here's the data to prove it..."

**If Too Inhibitory (boring/dry):**
- Add personal story (HEART axis)
- Add urgency statement (PSYCHE axis)
- Increase pacing
- Example: "Now here's the data [MIND], but let me tell you why this made me so angry [HEART]..."

## KB-VIDEO-SEO-OPTIMIZATION-v1.0 (4-5 Pages)

### 1. YouTube SEO Architecture

**The YouTube Ranking Algorithm (2026):**

YouTube ranking = CTR × Retention × Satisfaction × Recency

**Component 1: CTR (Click-Through Rate)**
- **Primary Drivers:** Thumbnail + Title
- **Target:** 6%+ for established channels, 4%+ for new channels
- **Optimization:**
  - Title: Front-load primary keyword, keep <60 chars
  - Thumbnail: Use one of 4 stun types, 3-hook alignment
  - A/B test thumbnails after first 48 hours if CTR <4%

**Component 2: Retention (Average View Duration)**
- **Primary Drivers:** Hook quality + Grease-slide connectors
- **Target:** 50%+ AVD for videos <10 min, 40%+ for videos >10 min
- **Optimization:**
  - Hook must deliver EVR gap 7+
  - Insert rehooks every 90-120 seconds
  - Check retention graph for cliffs, fix with pattern interrupts

**Component 3: Satisfaction (Engagement Signals)**
- **Primary Drivers:** Likes, comments, shares, saves
- **Target:** 4%+ like rate, 0.5%+ comment rate
- **Optimization:**
  - Ask specific question in video ("Comment below: which tool do you currently use?")
  - Pin your own comment with CTA immediately after publishing
  - Respond to first 20 comments within 2 hours (boosts early engagement)

**Component 4: Recency**
- **Primary Drivers:** Topic freshness, upload consistency
- **Target:** Include year in title (2026), mention recent events
- **Optimization:**
  - Update thumbnails/titles of older videos annually
  - Reference recent industry changes in intro

### 2. AI Discovery Optimization

**Why It Matters:** Gemini, Perplexity, and ChatGPT cite YouTube videos 3.2x more than in 2024. Video is now a primary source for LLMs.

**Citation Magnet Strategy for Video:**

**Tactic 1: Named Frameworks**
- **What:** Give your methodology a specific name
- **Why:** AI can cite "The MMA Framework" but can't cite "a good approach"
- **How:** Create 2-4 word name, display on screen, verbalize clearly
- **Example:** Instead of "here's my process," say "This is what I call the BOFU Filter Framework"

**Tactic 2: Data Tables on Screen**
- **What:** Visual data that AI can OCR (Optical Character Recognition)
- **Why:** YouTube's AI extracts text from frames, indexes it in transcript
- **How:** Display comparison tables, pricing grids, before/after metrics for 8-10 seconds
- **Example:** Show side-by-side tool comparison with features, pricing, ratings

**Tactic 3: Verbatim Quotes**
- **What:** Speak complete, citation-worthy sentences
- **Why:** AI prefers complete thoughts with subject-verb-object structure
- **How:** Write script in full sentences, avoid fragmented speech
- **Bad Example:** "So like, it's better because, you know, the thing is faster"
- **Good Example:** "Tool A processes 10,000 keywords in 3 minutes, while Tool B takes 47 minutes for the same task."

**Tactic 4: Chapter-Based Q&A**
- **What:** Structure chapters as questions AI users ask
- **Why:** When someone asks "How do I audit technical SEO?" AI searches for that exact phrase in chapters
- **How:** Name chapters as questions, not labels
- **Bad Example:** "Introduction," "Step 1," "Conclusion"
- **Good Example:** "What is technical SEO?" "How do I audit crawl budget?" "What tools are needed?"

### 3. Transcript Optimization

**The Transcript SEO Opportunity:** YouTube auto-generates transcripts. Google indexes them. AI reads them.

**Transcript Optimization Rules:**

**Rule 1: Speak Your Keywords**
- Don't just show keyword on screen—say it out loud
- Repeat primary keyword 5-8 times in a 10-minute video
- Use semantic variations (e.g., "keyword research tools," "tools for keyword analysis," "keyword discovery software")

**Rule 2: Minimize Filler Words**
- AI discounts sentences with high filler word ratios
- Target: <5% filler words (um, uh, like, you know)
- How: Script your video, practice 2-3 times before recording
- Use: Descript or Adobe Podcast to auto-remove filler in edit

**Rule 3: Complete Sentences**
- AI parses complete grammatical structures better than fragments
- Bad: "So basically the thing with keywords is um they're important"
- Good: "Keywords are the foundation of SEO. Targeting the right keywords determines whether you get traffic or waste months of work."

**Rule 4: Define Technical Terms**
- When you use jargon, define it immediately
- Why: Creates semantic richness AI uses for context
- Example: "We use BOFU keywords—that's Bottom of Funnel, high-purchase-intent search terms—to target buyers, not browsers."

**Rule 5: Chapter Timestamps in Description**
- YouTube extracts chapter titles into transcript metadata
- Include primary keyword in first 3 chapter titles
- Format:
  ```
  0:00 What is technical SEO for SaaS?
  1:47 How to audit technical SEO in 15 minutes
  3:22 Best technical SEO tools for SaaS (2026)
  ```

### 5. Video-to-Article Integration (Hub-and-Spoke)

**The Hub-and-Spoke Video Strategy:**

**Hub = Video (Engagement + Trust)**
- Primary content format
- Hosted on YouTube
- Optimized for retention and CTR
- Builds E-E-A-T through human presenter

**Spoke 1 = Blog Article (Depth + SEO)**
- Embeds video at top
- Expands on video points with 2,000-3,000 words
- Includes FAQ schema targeting related keywords
- Optimized for "read more" searches

**Spoke 2 = Social Clips (Distribution)**
- 60-90 second clips extracted from video
- Different hooks for LinkedIn, X, TikTok
- Links back to full video

**Spoke 3 = Email Sequence (Nurture)**
- Video linked in email
- Positions video as "exclusive training"
- CTA to watch + download resource

**Integration Workflow:**

```
Week 1 Day 1: Record video
Week 1 Day 2: Edit video, extract 3 best clips
Week 1 Day 3: Publish video to YouTube with schema
Week 1 Day 4: Publish blog article embedding video
Week 1 Day 5: Publish social clips linking to video
Week 1 Day 6: Send email featuring video
Week 1 Day 7: Analyze first-week data, adjust thumbnails if needed
```

**Bi-Directional Linking:**
- Video description links to blog article ("Read the full guide: [link]")
- Blog article embeds video at top ("Watch the video version")
- Social clips link to both video and article
- Creates link equity flow between owned properties
