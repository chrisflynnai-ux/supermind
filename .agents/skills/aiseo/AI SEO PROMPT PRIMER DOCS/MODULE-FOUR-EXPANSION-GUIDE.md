1. **EVR Psychology (Expectations vs Reality)** (2 pages)
   - **Definition**: Retention engineering through managing viewer expectations
   - **The EVR Gap**: Widen the gap between what they expect and what you deliver
   - **Setup in first 15 seconds**: "You think X, but actually Y"
   - **Payoff in body**: Gradually close the gap with pattern recognition
   - **Examples**:
     - Expectation: "SEO is about keywords"
     - Reality: "SEO is about making AI want to quote you"
     - Gap: How do you bridge keyword obsession → citation magnets?
   - **EVR Scoring Rubric**:
     - 0-3: No gap (boring, expected)
     - 4-6: Small gap (mildly interesting)
     - 7-8: Clear gap (viewer stays to see resolution)
     - 9-10: Massive gap (viral potential, "wait, what?")

### KB-RETENTION-MECHANICS-v1.0 (5-6 pages)

**Content to include:**

1. **The Script Workflow (Write in This Order)** (1.5 pages)
   - **Step 1: Psychology** - Identify the EVR gap, neuro-axes to trigger
   - **Step 2: Packaging** - Design thumbnail + title BEFORE writing script
   - **Step 3: Outline** - Pass the "Uniqueness Gate" (if not novel, research more)
   - **Step 4: Intro** - Hook + Pattern Interrupt + Thesis (write this second-to-last)
   - **Step 5: Body** - Write this FIRST (main value, easiest to write)
   - **Step 6: Outro** - Fortune cookie + Native CTA (write this last)
   - **Why this order**: Body establishes what you're actually delivering (reality), then Intro creates expectation gap, then Outro resolves

2. **Body Sequencing: Rising Pattern Recognition** (2 pages)
   - **Pattern Recognition Definition**: Viewer's growing ability to predict what's next
   - **The Goal**: Make each point slightly more advanced than the last
   - **Sequencing Purchase Drivers in Video**:
     - **Minute 1-3**: BODY/Safety anchor ("You're safe here, I'm not selling you")
     - **Minute 3-5**: HEART/Emotional activation ("This will make you feel X")
     - **Minute 5-8**: MIND/Logic layering ("Here's why this works")
     - **Minute 8-10**: SPIRIT/Social proof ("Others are doing this")
     - **Minute 10-12**: PSYCHE/Action trigger ("Here's your next step")
     - **Minute 12-end**: CONSCIENCE/Belonging ("You're now part of X group")
   - **Rehook Strategy**: Every 90-120 seconds, remind viewer of the EVR gap
     - "Remember, we're trying to solve [original problem]..."
     - "This is the part that changes everything..."
     - "Here's where it gets interesting..."
   - **Balance Checking for Video**:
     - Count excitatory moments (BODY/MIND/PSYCHE)
     - Count inhibitory moments (HEART/SPIRIT/CONSCIENCE)
     - Max difference: 3-4 points (same as written content)
     - Example: 8 excitatory moments, 5 inhibitory moments = 3-point gap (acceptable)

3. **Grease-Slide Connectors (20+ Examples)** (1.5 pages)

   **Definition**: Transition phrases that make viewer unable to stop watching

   **Tier 1: Mystery Escalation**
   - "But it gets worse..."
   - "Here's the part no one tells you..."
   - "This is where things get weird..."
   - "Wait until you see what happened next..."
   - "And then I discovered something..."

   **Tier 2: Pattern Breaks**
   - "Everything I just said? Ignore it. Here's why..."
   - "Now flip that on its head..."
   - "This is the exception to the rule..."
   - "Here's where conventional wisdom fails..."

   **Tier 3: Social Proof Nudges**
   - "The top 1% do this differently..."
   - "I asked 50 [experts] and they all said..."
   - "The companies paying $X/month know..."

   **Tier 4: Urgency Injections**
   - "This won't work if you skip the next part..."
   - "The window for this is closing because..."
   - "By the time you finish this video..."

   **Tier 5: Curiosity Loops**
   - "I'll show you the proof in a second, but first..."
   - "Remember this number: [X]. It'll matter in a minute..."
   - "There are 3 reasons this works. #1 is obvious, #2 is subtle, #3 will surprise you..."

4. **Visual Retention Aids** (1 page)
   - **B-roll Timing**: Change visual every 3-5 seconds (attention span limit)
   - **On-screen Text**: Reinforce spoken hook (3-hook alignment)
   - **Graphics/Charts**: Use for MIND/Logic moments (data visualization)

   - **Face Cam Moments**: Use for HEART/Emotional + BODY/Safety (human connection)
